Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our legal services and processes

How long does the probate process typically take in Oregon?

The probate process in Oregon typically takes 6-12 months, depending on the complexity of the estate, whether there are disputes among beneficiaries, and the efficiency of the court system. Simple estates may be completed faster, while complex estates with significant assets or family disputes may take longer.

What should I bring to my initial consultation?

For your initial consultation, please bring any relevant documents related to your case, such as court papers, contracts, wills, financial records, or correspondence. Also, prepare a list of questions and concerns you'd like to discuss. Don't worry if you don't have all documents - we can help you identify what's needed.

How are legal fees structured for different types of cases?

Our fee structure varies by case type. Probate and estate administration typically use hourly billing. Family law cases may use hourly rates or flat fees depending on complexity. Criminal defense often involves flat fees based on the charges. We'll discuss fees transparently during your consultation.

Do you handle cases outside of Oregon?

Our practice is focused on Oregon state law and courts. However, we may be able to assist with certain matters that have Oregon connections or work with attorneys in other states when appropriate. We'll discuss this during your consultation if it applies to your situation.

What makes Talbert Law Firm different from other firms?

Our firm combines extensive legal expertise with genuine personal attention. We limit our caseload to ensure each client receives the time and focus they deserve. Our diverse practice areas allow us to handle multiple aspects of your legal needs, and our compassionate approach helps guide you through difficult times.
